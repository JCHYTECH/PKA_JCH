Yes, technically possible, but with constraints worth understanding before we build it in.

**What works:**
- In the **HTML/Ghost version**: straightforward. An `<img>` tag pulls the cover from a public URL (publisher site, Amazon, etc.). No issue.
- In the **PDF version**: also possible — I fetch or you supply the cover image, I embed it next to the review title.

**The constraints:**

1. **Copyright** — book cover images are copyrighted by the publisher/designer. Embedding them in a publication (even editorial/review) sits in a grey zone. Editorial use in press reviews is generally tolerated in practice, but it is not clean legally without explicit permission. Arteon should be aware of this.

2. **Image sourcing** — I cannot fetch images from the web directly into the PDF at build time (no outbound network in the build environment). Two options:
   - You supply the cover images as uploads
   - I use the publisher's official press/media page URLs in the HTML version only, with a note that PDF embedding requires manual addition

3. **Layout impact** — covers are typically portrait-oriented. I'd place them as a small thumbnail (approx. 45×60mm) floated to the right of the review title block. Clean, editorial, not dominant.

**My recommendation:**
- HTML version: covers pulled from publisher URLs, displayed at thumbnail scale with proper credit line
- PDF version: placeholder blocks indicating where to drop cover images, with the exact source URLs listed
