## Assistant
*2026-02-21 21:40*

Yes. You can use the **Laowa 25mm f/2.8 2.5–5X Ultra Macro (Canon EF mount)** on your **Canon R10** with the **Canon EF–RF adapter**. There is no technical incompatibility.

However, there are important operational considerations, especially given your interest in high-magnification macro and focus stacking.

First, compatibility. The Laowa 25mm 2.5–5X is a fully manual lens: no electronic contacts, no autofocus, no aperture control from the body. The Canon EF–RF adapter is purely mechanical in this case. The R10 will work in:
• Manual (M) mode  
• Aperture Priority (Av), though aperture is set manually on the lens  

Enable “Release shutter without lens” in the R10 menu if needed.

Second, optical and mechanical behavior at 2.5–5X. This lens is not a general macro lens like your Canon 100mm. It is a high-magnification probe-style macro requiring very short working distances:

• At 2.5X: working distance ≈ 45 mm  
• At 5X: working distance ≈ 40 mm  
• Effective aperture becomes much smaller due to magnification (f/2.8 behaves closer to f/8–f/16 in practice)

This means:
• Very strong light loss  
• Extremely shallow depth of field  
• Flash or strong continuous lighting is practically mandatory  

On APS-C (R10), you get slightly tighter framing than on full frame, which is often beneficial for insects.

Third, focus stacking implications. You are already considering motorized rails. With this Laowa, focus is achieved mainly by changing subject distance, not by turning the focus ring (which adjusts magnification). Therefore:

For stacking:
• Lock magnification (e.g., 3X or 4X)  
• Move camera with a motorized rail (good alignment)  
• Use small incremental steps (typically 10–40 microns depending on aperture)

Given your 2 kg rail load assumption discussed earlier, this lens is perfectly suited to a motorized stacking setup.

Fourth, adapter choice. Prefer:
• Canon EF–EOS R official adapter (no optics, just spacer)  
• Avoid cheap adapters with play; at 5X, any flex introduces alignment error  

There is no need for a control-ring adapter since the lens is fully manual.

Fifth, practical warning. This lens is optimized for 2.5–5X only. It does not focus to infinity. It is a specialized instrument for:
• Insect eyes  
• Texture studies  
• Fine structures  

It is not a replacement for your 100mm macro.

Conclusion. Technically: yes, fully compatible. Practically: it becomes a precision laboratory optic requiring rail, flash control, and stable support. In your workflow (macro insects + stacking), it makes sense as a complementary high-magnification tool rather than a primary macro lens.
