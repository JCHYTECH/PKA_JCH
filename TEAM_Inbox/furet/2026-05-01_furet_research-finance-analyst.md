## ROLE RESEARCH BRIEF

**Target expertise:** Finance & Investment Analysis — startup/SME context, shareholder agreements, cap tables, deal economics

---

### What this person actually does day-to-day

- Reads financial statements and extracts what matters: burn rate, margins, valuation drivers, runway
- Models investment scenarios: returns, dilution, cap table evolution across funding rounds
- Analyzes the economic terms of shareholder agreements — liquidation preferences, anti-dilution, drag-along, ratchets — not the legal form (that's [[Renard]]), but what they mean in cash at exit
- Structures go/no-go memos: risk-adjusted return, strategic fit, downside scenarios, open questions
- Translates complex financial instruments into plain language for decision-making
- Works with term sheets, investment memos, pitch decks, audited accounts, statutory filings

---

### Core [[knowledge]] required

- Corporate finance: DCF, comparable transactions, EV/EBITDA, revenue multiples, IRR
- Startup financing mechanics: pre-seed to Series B, SAFE notes, convertible bonds, equity rounds, option pools
- Cap table modeling: dilution simulation, pro-rata rights, waterfall analysis at various exit prices
- Shareholder agreement economics: participating vs. non-participating preferences, full ratchet vs. broad-based weighted average anti-dilution, earnouts
- Accounting literacy: P&L, balance sheet, cash flow — diagnostic, not forensic
- Belgian/French corporate context: SRL/SA structures, droit des sociétés, working alongside [[Renard]]

---

### Adjacent [[knowledge]] that separates good from great

- Qualitative risk modeling: team quality signals, market timing, competitive moat assessment
- Recognizing when NOT to invest: toxic terms, cap table red flags, founder dynamics issues
- Reading between the lines of a pitch deck — what is missing, what is oversold, what the numbers don't show
- Knowing when to escalate to external expertise: auditor, notaire, sector specialist
- Understanding minority investor position — rights, protections, and their limits

---

### Common blind spots to avoid

- Confusing legal risk and financial risk — [[Renard]] holds legal; this specialist holds financial
- Over-modeling: a 50-tab model is not a decision — a clear memo with a recommendation is
- Ignoring qualitative signals in favor of pure numbers
- Forgetting that JCH is typically a minority investor — minority protections and information rights must always be checked
